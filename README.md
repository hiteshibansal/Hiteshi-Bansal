# Nestlé India Ltd — Financial Performance Analysis (FY2015–FY2026)

A business analytics project analysing Nestlé India Ltd's standalone financial performance using **Excel**, **R**, and real financial data, with a written analytical report.

## 📌 Project Overview

This project examines 12 years of Nestlé India's financials (FY2015–FY2026) to understand revenue and profit trends, margin stability, return on capital (ROCE), the impact of a 2024 capex expansion cycle, and working capital efficiency.

**Data source:** [Screener.in](https://www.screener.in/company/NESTLEIND/) (standalone financials, compiled from Nestlé India's audited annual reports and BSE/NSE filings). Figures in ₹ Crores.

## 🔍 Key Insight
Nestlé India changed its financial year-end from December to March in 2024, creating a one-time **15-month transition period**. This is a critical analytical caveat — much like a corporate restructuring — that affects year-on-year comparability and is explicitly handled throughout the analysis (excluded from CAGR/trend calculations, flagged in every relevant chart and table).

## 🛠️ Tools & Techniques

| Tool | What it was used for |
|---|---|
| **Excel** | 6-sheet financial model (Read Me, P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, Ratios, Dashboard). All ratios computed via live cross-sheet formulas (Net Profit Margin, ROE, Debt-to-Equity, Interest Coverage, Asset Turnover, EPS growth, FCF margin) — nothing hardcoded. 2×2 chart dashboard. |
| **R** (base R only) | Year-on-year growth rates & CAGR, descriptive statistics, Pearson correlation analysis (including a full-period vs. sub-period comparison that reveals a regime change), and an OLS linear regression revenue forecast. |

## 📈 Key Findings
- Revenue CAGR of 11.2% and Net Profit CAGR of 23.3% (FY2015–FY2023, clean 12-month years)
- Operating margin stable at ~22.5% average (SD 1.75) — strong pricing power
- ROCE peaked at 169% (FY2024) then fell to 84% (FY2026) as capex roughly doubled — capex vs. ROCE correlation is weak over the full period (r = 0.14) but strongly negative in the FY23–FY26 sub-period (r = −0.80)
- Structurally negative Cash Conversion Cycle (as low as −24 days) — suppliers effectively fund working capital
